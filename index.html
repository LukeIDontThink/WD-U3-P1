Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi was a key figure in India's struggle for independence from British colonial rule. He employed nonviolent civil disobedience to lead a movement that eventually led to India's freedom. Gandhi's principles of nonviolence and truth continue to inspire movements for civil rights and freedom worldwide. His leadership and philosophy made a profound impact on social and political change.

Malala Yousafzai

Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani activist and advocate for girls' education. She defied the Taliban's ban on girls attending school in her region and became an international symbol of girls' rights to education. Malala survived an assassination attempt and continued to champion education for girls globally. She is the youngest-ever Nobel Prize laureate and a role model for her unwavering dedication to education and gender equality.
