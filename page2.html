What They Did:

Mahatma Gandhi, often referred to as the "Father of the Nation" in India, was a prominent leader and activist. He dedicated his life to the struggle for India's independence from British colonial rule. Gandhi's approach was rooted in nonviolent civil disobedience, which he called "Satyagraha." He believed that peaceful resistance was the most potent weapon for oppressed people. Gandhi faced numerous challenges and obstacles throughout his life. He was arrested multiple times for his activism, and he endured imprisonment. He undertook hunger strikes to protest injustice and discrimination, putting his own health and life at risk.

Accomplishments:

Gandhi's leadership and philosophy led to numerous significant accomplishments. His influence was instrumental in gaining India's independence from British rule in 1947. He inspired movements and individuals across the globe to employ nonviolent resistance as a means to bring about social and political change. Gandhi's teachings of truth, nonviolence, and tolerance continue to be studied and revered by people of various backgrounds and generations.
